WHAT IT IS
Vitar is a clinic management and appointment-scheduling platform built by
LiveVault, a Nigerian software company, for Nigerian private clinics.
It replaces the notebook-and-phone-calls way most small clinics run
bookings with one simple, Naira-priced online system.

THE PROBLEM
Private clinics in Nigeria lose real money to:
- Missed appointments (patients forget, or were never reminded)
- Manual, error-prone booking by phone or notebook
- Double-booked doctors and empty slots that go unnoticed until month-end
- No easy way to see which patients are likely to skip their next visit
- Paper patient records that slow down check-in and follow-up

THE SOLUTION
Vitar gives a clinic one dashboard to run its front desk from:
- Patients book online in under a minute — no app download required
- Automatic WhatsApp, SMS, and email reminders bring patients back on time
- No-show risk scoring flags which patients staff should call first
- QR code check-in pulls up a patient's full history instantly
- Analytics show bookings and attendance trends every month, so a clinic
  owner isn't guessing where revenue is leaking

Industry benchmarks for automated reminders suggest clinics can cut
no-shows by up to 40% — that's the core promise Vitar is built around.

WHO IT'S FOR
Nigerian private clinics — from a solo GP with one or two doctors to
multi-branch hospital groups. Right now, Vitar is focused specifically on
individual clinic decision-makers in Nigeria, not a broad African market.

HOW IT WORKS
For patients: pick a doctor and time online -> get confirmed -> get
reminded automatically -> show up.
For clinic staff: set up the clinic profile once -> book and let Vitar
handle reminders -> track attendance and risk in one dashboard.
No new hardware. It runs in a browser on any phone, tablet, or laptop
staff already use.
